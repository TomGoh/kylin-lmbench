# pKVM overhead on Phytium FTC862 — day 2: signal converged

**Date**: 2026-06-02
**Target**: N90 (Phytium FTC862, 8 cores, KylinOS V11, kernel 6.6.0-52-generic-pkvm10)
**Day-1 doc**: `docs/findings-2026-06-01.md` — methodology evolution + initial signals

## TL;DR

Three rounds of boot-time variable elimination produced a **five-way N=5
dataset with MAD% ≤ 2.8% everywhere** that disambiguates the previously-
confounded signals into **four independent findings**:

1. **pKVM imposes essentially zero overhead on host syscalls.**
   `pkvm-host` and `baremetal` (same kernel, only difference is
   `kvm-arm.mode=protected` in cmdline) are indistinguishable on every
   benchmark (±2% within MAD).

2. **pKVM imposes +4-8% overhead on guest stage-2 / IPC / fork operations.**
   With LSM removed on both sides to neutralize confounders, `baremetal+noLSM`
   and `pkvm-guest-np+noLSM` differ by +6.6% on `lat_proc/fork`, +6.2% on
   `lat_unix/rt`, +8.0% on `lat_pipe/rt`, +4.1% on `lat_pagefault/minor`,
   +3.8% on `lat_proc/exec`. Naked syscalls (null/read/write/stat/sig-install)
   are equal to within ±1%. `tlb/effective = 48 pages` identical across all
   four configurations.

3. **The Kylin KYSEC `ksaf` LSM costs ~134 cycles per syscall on this host.**
   Removing `ksaf` from the LSM stack on baremetal drops `lat_syscall null`
   from 152 ns to 88 ns (−42%, ≈64 ns per syscall ≈ 134 cycles at 2.1 GHz).
   This is **larger in magnitude than the pKVM tax** and is an independent,
   paper-worthy KylinOS-specific finding.

4. **`kvm-arm.mode=nvhe` adds a flat +16% to every syscall vs the default
   (VHE) mode — independent of pKVM and LSM.** Forcing the kernel into
   non-VHE KVM mode costs ~32 cycles per syscall on every benchmark
   uniformly. The implication: literature comparisons of "pKVM vs vanilla
   KVM" that use NVHE as the vanilla baseline are implicitly comparing
   against an already-+16%-penalized state. Phytium's default mode is VHE,
   and pKVM is built on top of VHE, so pKVM-host syscall fastpath inherits
   the VHE fast path with no additional cost.

## The five configurations

All on N90 cpu0 (vCPU0 pinned to host cpu0 for guest), locked 2.1 GHz,
THP=never, ASLR=0, sched_autogroup=0, kernel 6.6.0-52-generic.
Bench: CONFIG.test, N=5 iters.

| ENV_TAG | side | cmdline (modulo defaults) | LSM stack | KYSEC userspace |
|---------|------|---------------------------|-----------|-----------------|
| `pkvm-host` | **host** | `... kvm-arm.mode=protected security=box lsm=ksaf audit=1` | capability,kycp,ksaf | most active |
| `baremetal` | **host** | `... security=box lsm=ksaf audit=1` (no kvm-arm.mode → default VHE) | capability,kycp,ksaf | most active |
| `baremetal+noLSM` | **host** | `... security= lsm= audit=0` (no kvm-arm.mode → default VHE) | **capability,kycp only** | **all masked** |
| `nvhe+noLSM` | **host** | `... security= lsm= audit=0 kvm-arm.mode=nvhe` | capability,kycp | masked |
| `guest-noLSM` | **guest** (pKVM non-protected, crosvm-launched) | `... lsm= audit=0` inside guest, host stays in pKVM mode | capability,kycp | mostly absent |

Switching between cmdline variants was done by adding new BLS entries to
`/boot/grub/grub.cfg` and using `grub-reboot <index>` for one-shot next-boot
selection — entries kept around for reproducibility (see Methods below).

## Reading the four columns

### Finding 1: pKVM is free on host syscalls

Column `pkvm-host` vs `baremetal` — identical hardware, identical kernel,
identical userspace, identical LSM, **only** cmdline difference is
`kvm-arm.mode=protected`:

- Every syscall metric within ±2% of the other (= within MAD noise floor)
- `tlb/effective`, `lat_pagefault`, `lat_proc fork` all within ±2%

Interpretation: **the EL2-resident pKVM hypervisor does not measurably
extend the host's EL0→EL1 syscall path** on Phytium FTC862. The kernel
patches for pKVM host support are well-isolated from the host-syscall
fastpath.

### Finding 2: pKVM tax on the guest, cleanly isolated

Column `bm+noLSM` vs `gst-noLSM` — both have *only* `capability,kycp` LSM,
no audit, no KYSEC daemons (host has them masked, guest has them effectively
inert without ksaf module). This is the cleanest possible Linux-vs-pKVM-guest
comparison.

| operation class | guest tax | mechanism |
|-----------------|----------:|-----------|
| Naked syscalls (null/read/write/stat/fstat/sig-install) | **±1%** | no measurable cost |
| `lat_sig/catch` | +1.5% | minimal |
| `lat_pagefault/minor` | **+4.1%** | first-touch may need stage-2 PTE alloc |
| `lat_proc/exec` | **+3.8%** | mm replacement under stage-2 |
| `lat_proc/fork` | **+6.6%** | mm copy → many stage-2 entries |
| `lat_unix/rt` | **+6.2%** | IPC across guest kernel + EL2 IRQ |
| `lat_pipe/rt` | **+8.0%** | same, possibly worse due to wake path |
| `tlb/effective` | **0%** | hardware TLB capacity unchanged |

Stage-2 / IPC / fork operations consistently pay 4-8%. Pure userspace work
and naked syscalls are free. This is the "production-grade pKVM tax" — and
notably it is **lower than reported in early pKVM literature** (which
typically cited 10-30% on fork-heavy workloads).

Anomaly: `lat_sig/prot` (mprotect) shows guest *slower* than baremetal by
about 10% (0.141 vs 0.127 µs). MAD% is 2.8 on the baremetal side so the
sign is significant, but the direction is inconsistent with the other
stage-2 metrics. Worth a focused re-run.

### Finding 3: KYSEC `ksaf` LSM costs 134 cycles per syscall on this host

Column `pkvm-host` vs `bm+noLSM` — same hardware, same kernel image, same
THP/ASLR state. Difference: full KYSEC LSM stack
(`capability,kycp,ksaf` plus userspace daemons) vs minimal stack
(`capability,kycp` plus no userspace):

| benchmark | with full KYSEC | without ksaf | delta |
|-----------|----------------:|-------------:|------:|
| `lat_syscall null` | 0.1524 µs | 0.0884 µs | **−42% (≈64 ns / 134 cycles)** |
| `lat_syscall read` | 0.2044 | 0.1403 | −31% |
| `lat_syscall write` | 0.1853 | 0.1206 | −35% |
| `lat_syscall stat` | 1.1089 | 0.8254 | −26% |
| `lat_syscall open` | 1.8007 | 1.2655 | −30% |
| `lat_syscall fstat` | 0.2566 | 0.1966 | −23% |
| `lat_sig install` | 0.1903 | 0.1265 | −34% |
| `lat_sig catch` | 0.9000 | 0.7768 | −14% |
| `lat_pipe rt` | 5.6438 | 4.9943 | −12% |
| `lat_proc fork` | 120.72 | 115.58 | −4% |
| `lat_proc shell` | 730.38 | 688.13 | −6% |

The absolute saving on simple syscalls is **~64 ns per syscall** —
consistent across null/read/write/install. This is the per-invocation cost
of traversing the `ksaf` LSM hook chain. Larger syscalls (stat/open) show
proportionally smaller delta because their fixed setup cost dominates over
the per-syscall ksaf overhead.

This is independent of the pKVM measurement and would still hold if pKVM
were not in the picture. It is a useful data point for KylinOS engineering
("`ksaf` adds 134 cycles to every syscall on Phytium").

### Finding 4: explicit `kvm-arm.mode=nvhe` adds +16% to every syscall

Column `bm+noLSM` (kernel default — no `kvm-arm.mode` flag) vs `nvhe+noLSM`
(same in every other way, just adds `kvm-arm.mode=nvhe` to cmdline). All
other variables held constant: same kernel binary, same LSM-off state,
same THP/ASLR, same masked daemons.

| benchmark | bm+noLSM (default = VHE) | nvhe+noLSM | nvhe penalty |
|-----------|-------------------------:|-----------:|-------------:|
| `lat_syscall null` | 0.0884 µs | 0.1031 µs | **+16.6%** (+32 cyc) |
| `lat_syscall read` | 0.1403 | 0.1650 | +17.6% |
| `lat_syscall write` | 0.1206 | 0.1407 | +16.7% |
| `lat_syscall stat` | 0.8254 | 0.9622 | +16.6% |
| `lat_syscall open` | 1.2655 | 1.4782 | +16.8% |
| `lat_syscall fstat` | 0.1966 | 0.2274 | +15.7% |
| `lat_sig install` | 0.1265 | 0.1468 | +16.0% |
| `lat_sig catch` | 0.7768 | 0.9026 | +16.2% |
| `lat_sig prot` | 0.1411 | 0.1640 | +16.2% |
| `lat_pipe rt` | 4.9943 | 5.7287 | +14.7% |
| `lat_unix rt` | 8.7145 | 10.1337 | +16.3% |
| `lat_pagefault minor` | 0.2120 | 0.2450 | +15.6% |
| `lat_proc fork` | 115.58 | 133.90 | +15.9% |
| `lat_proc exec` | 325.82 | 377.20 | +15.8% |
| `lat_proc shell` | 688.13 | 792.43 | +15.2% |
| `tlb effective` | 48 | 48 | 0% |

The penalty is **remarkably uniform — ~16% across every metric**.
The absolute saving on `lat_syscall null` is ~15 ns ≈ 32 cycles per
syscall, which is consistent with the cost of one extra EL transition
or one extra branch mispredict in the syscall vector path.

Interpretation: on Phytium FTC862 with kernel 6.6.0-52, **the default
KVM mode (no `kvm-arm.mode` flag) is VHE** (host kernel running with
EL2 privileges via the ARMv8.1 VHE feature). Explicit `kvm-arm.mode=nvhe`
forces traditional split-mode (host at EL1, hypervisor stub at EL2),
which inserts an extra EL transition step in the syscall entry/exit
path and costs ~32 cycles per syscall — regardless of whether any
virtualization is actually in use.

`pkvm-host` is built on top of VHE (host runs at EL2 with the additional
protected-guest support), so it inherits the VHE fast-path — this is
why finding 1 holds.

Implication for the pKVM-vs-vanilla-KVM literature: papers that report
"pKVM costs X% vs vanilla KVM" using NVHE as the vanilla baseline are
implicitly attributing the NVHE penalty to pKVM. The actual pKVM-vs-VHE
delta on host fastpath is **zero** (finding 1). The early-literature
10-30% gap was likely a sum of (a) NVHE baseline penalty (~16% here),
(b) pKVM guest stage-2 tax (~5-7% here for fork/IPC), and (c) less
mature pKVM kernel patches. The Kylin 6.6.0-pkvm10 build clearly
attributes overhead correctly.

## Caveat — what the guest noLSM column actually lacks

When the guest boots with `lsm= audit=0`, the `ksaf` module does not load.
However, the guest still has KYSEC *userspace* daemons running
(`ksaf-label-manager`, `kyseclogd`, `ksaf-devctl-sync-daemon` etc.) because
mkrootfs cloned the host's `/etc/systemd/system/*.wants` symlinks. These
daemons start, try to talk to `/sys/fs/box` / ksaf netlink, fail silently,
and idle. Their existence does not affect benchmarks (no per-syscall hook
without the kernel module) but they do consume some idle CPU.

The host `bm+noLSM` column has those same services **masked** via
`systemctl mask`, so they are not even started. Either way the per-syscall
LSM hook path is the same (the modules are simply absent).

## Methods additions (beyond day-1)

- `guest/launch-guest.sh` — added `--no-lsm` flag that replaces
  `security=box lsm=ksaf audit=1` in the guest cmdline with `lsm= audit=0`.
- `quiet-host.sh` — hard guard against ever including network-stack
  services in the stop list (we learned this the hard way by stopping
  NetworkManager and losing SSH on N90).
- Three new BLS menu entries persisted in N90's `/boot/grub/grub.cfg`:
  - `[BAREMETAL pkvm-off]` — same kernel/cmdline as default 6.6.0-52 entry
    but with `kvm-arm.mode=protected` stripped.
  - `[BAREMETAL + NO-LSM]` — additionally has `security= lsm= audit=0`.
  - `[KVM-NVHE noLSM]` — same as NO-LSM but adds `kvm-arm.mode=nvhe`.
  - One-shot selection via `sudo grub-reboot <index>` (`1>3` for
    BAREMETAL, `1>4` for NO-LSM, `1>5` for KVM-NVHE). Backup at
    `/boot/grub/grub.cfg.pre-baremetal-bench` (and `.pre-noLSM`, `.pre-nvhe`).
- `systemctl mask` on the KYSEC userspace daemons so they remain inactive
  after reboot without needing to be stopped each time.

## Open questions / next steps

- **CONFIG.host (full lmbench suite)** — repeat the 4-way comparison on the
  full suite (memory size sweep, BENCHMARK_FILE on virtio-blk for
  bounce-buffer testing). ~10 min per config × 4 configs × N=5 ≈ 3.5 hours.
- **pKVM protected guest (`--protected-vm-without-firmware`)** — measure
  the bounce-buffer cost on top of `pkvm-guest-np`. Likely the biggest
  open delta.
- **Multi-vCPU guest** — current data is single-vCPU. fork+IPC at 2 vCPUs
  may show different patterns due to cross-vCPU stage-2 invalidations.
- **`lat_sig/prot` anomaly** — mprotect direction inconsistent with other
  stage-2 metrics; warrants a focused re-run with isolated mprotect
  microbench.
- **TLB pressure under wide working sets** — `tlb effective` was 48 across
  all 4 configs but only at small workloads. Re-test with larger
  `lat_mem_rd` to detect stage-2 walk contention.

## What goes into the paper

A clean Methods + Results narrative for this dataset:

1. **Methods**: same as day-1, plus the boot-time variable elimination
   via custom BLS entries. Cite the four-way comparison.
2. **Results**: four findings clearly separated.
   - Figure 1: pKVM host tax = 0% (column `pkvm-host` vs `baremetal`).
   - Figure 2: pKVM guest tax = 4-8% on stage-2 paths (column `bm+noLSM`
     vs `gst-noLSM`).
   - Figure 3: KYSEC ksaf tax = 134 cycles per syscall (column `pkvm-host`
     vs `bm+noLSM`).
   - Figure 4: NVHE vs VHE host tax = +16% per syscall, uniform
     (column `bm+noLSM` vs `nvhe+noLSM`).
3. **Discussion**: the headline is *production pKVM has matured to the
   point where its overhead is dominated by per-VM stage-2 work, not by
   host-side hypervisor residence*. The orthogonal KYSEC observation is a
   bonus data point for KylinOS engineering. The NVHE finding is
   particularly relevant to the literature — it shows that
   "pKVM vs vanilla KVM" comparisons that use NVHE as the vanilla
   baseline are unfair to pKVM (which inherits the VHE fast path).
